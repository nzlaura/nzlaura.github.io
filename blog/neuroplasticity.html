Neuroplasticity and its benefits

Understanding what neuroplasticity is, and why it is helpful can help us to learn new things, and shape the pathways in our brain positively. If we understand what neuroplasticity is and how we can increase it, it can not only make us more effective learners, but inform the way we approach our learning.

In my research on neuroplasticity, I came across the idea that the way each of us learn is highly individual, and that patterns of neuroplasticity are different in every individual. A way that I could engage with this is to reflect on the ways that I learn best, and repeat those actions to increase my learning. I can also use positive habits and behaviours- such as exercise and journalling to shape my brain in positive ways. Because neuroplasticity can also be negative, so engaging in things that are beneficial to me can help boost my ability to engage with learning.
