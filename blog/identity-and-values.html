Identity and Values

April 2021

Situation where I had an ethical decision to make:

There are a few times in my life that I have face ethical decisions. For the most part, these decisions were in my work in mental health. This means that in order to protect the privacy of the people I worked with, it is difficult to share specific examples. I can reflect on the understanding of ethics in a professional setting that experience imparted in me though. Some of the ethical decisions I had to make reinforced one of my most important values- compassion. Showing compassion first to the clients that I worked with allowed us to resolve any issues more easily, especially when they concerned ethics and boundaries.

How my culture has influenced my values and identity:

I grew up in Auckland, in a fairly typical middle class pakeha family. I find it difficult to describe how my culture has impacted upon me because I feel that I grew up without any specific cultural influences. I have never considered before the kind of impact that culture has had on me. When I was working on this exercise I instead focused more on the values my family held influenced my identity. My parents placed a great deal of importance on effort over achievement, and on honesty. I feel like those values are good, because honesty has been a really important value for me through my life.

My strengths and limitations in my learning and career development

When I was gathering evidence for this blog, I took the myers briggs test through the 16personalities website. I have done the myers briggs test at other times in my life and it has consistently given me the "INFJ" result, or as the site describes, the advocate personality type. I explored the strengths and limitations associated with the INFJ type and I can identify with some of them.

Limitations: In my life I have felt sensitive to criticism. For me this creates limitations in terms of my career and learning because at times I avoid doing things or taking opportunities in order to avoid possible criticism. Another limitation I see in myself that can have an impact is that I struggle to ask for help when I come across roadblocks. I feel like in learning it is important to be able to ask for help when you need it. Finally, I feel like both of the limitations I have listed are driven by perfectionism. When I'm working on a task, I will often not want to submit it or show it to other people unless I can consider it perfect. This extends to reflections that I need to share with others- I worry that the way I reflect will be wrong.

Strengths: One of my strengths is that I value compassion. This is evidenced by my career in mental health- I wanted a career where my purpose was to support people and help improve their lives. My role was as a community support worker, and I helped peoplewith experiences of mental illness to achieve goals that improved their wellbeing in the community. I believe this will support me in learning and in my career because when I'm working with others, I consider it important to extend compassion to the people I am working and learning alongside.

Strengths that might help in my learning journey and how they might intersect with obstacles

I believe that patience is a strength that will help me with my learning journey. Patience will allow me to trust that learning is a process, and that given time I will be able to solve problems. In some ways this intersects with my issues with struggling to ask for help when I need it. Sometimes it might be time to stop being patient with a problem, and I may find it difficult to identify that.

An example of a time when I worked with others

When I was at university I worked as a resident adviser at the hall of residence where I lived. a specific example I can think of where I needed to work with others was area meetings. I met with residents twice every semester to discuss issues and and come together as a group socially. The students in my area didn't understand why leaving items in the hallway was a fire hazard.

We would have to bring this up regularly, and remind them to keep their personal belongings in their rooms. this created tension because they didn't understandth that it was my responsibility to have them follow the rules. At the time I used humour to diffuse the situation- once I made a lighthearted joke about the issue they seemed to understand a little better. Relfecting on that issue I think that in that setting I would have used the same tactic as it was appropriate to the audience. In the workplace I may have used a different tactic, but in the setting it was a good way to address that specific issue.
